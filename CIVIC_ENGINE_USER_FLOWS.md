## Key Design Decisions

### Privacy
- Teachers CAN see individual student responses, but they are **anonymized** for grading
- This lets teachers grade reasoning quality without knowing which student holds which position
- Students CAN see each other's discussion posts (by design - deliberation requires visibility)
- Aggregate class data is visible to all; individual positions are not

### Deliberation Structure
- **Steelmanning required**: Students must articulate the opposing view before submitting their position
- **Reasoning required**: No naked votes - every position needs justification
- **Revision tracked**: Position changes after discussion are logged (shows learning)
- **Reflection required**: Students must articulate what they learned

### Reading Levels
- Students CHOOSE their reading level (not forced into "6th grade language")
- Options: Simplified, Standard, Advanced
- "Dig deeper" expansions for those who want more

### Anti-Herding
- Students submit initial positions BEFORE seeing class aggregate
- Aggregate only revealed after submission
- Revision is a separate, explicit step

### Teacher Empowerment
- Teachers customize policy sets (not stuck with pre-made lists)
- Facilitation guides aligned with standards (C3 Framework)
- Analytics focus on participation quality, not position correctness
